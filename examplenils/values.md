### Spread good memes.

In the late 1970s, evolutionary biologist Richard Dawkins wrote a book called "The Selfish Gene" to describe the neo-Darwinian synthesis. The idea was that natural selection, the survival of the fittest, did not act on the level of the species, or the tribe ,or even the individual, but rather at the level of the gene: the true drama of nature was played out at the level of genes. And in this book he had a thought experiment: a chapter dedicated to the question "what if behaviors and cultures were subject to natural selection"? He coined the term "meme" to describe a unit of culture or behavior that could be copied through imitation. He explored how some memes could be more fit than others and you could observe that through watching how successfully they manage to reproduce through a cultural environment.

My value of "spread good memes" means that I want to be mindful of what memes I help propagate. It is a call to mindfulness in terms of what I say and what I amplify, what information I pass along, and what behaviors that I reinforce.

Above all else, spread good memes. Be the meme you want to see in the world.

### Increase intercognitive bandwidth

The most human thing of all is shared intersubjective moments - when we are an intercognitive entity, perceiving the world together through shared language and context.

Through my work at Auki I want to increase civilization's intercognitive bandwidth. The real world web will make it possible for us to communicate more clearly with each other (through shared augmented reality) and which machines (through shared spatial references). Spread good memes.
