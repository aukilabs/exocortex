I am the founder and CEO of Auki Labs.

Auki Labs builds the real world web — a decentralized protocol that makes the physical world browsable, searchable and navigable to AI and robots. We also build Cactus, an AI copilot for the retail industry.

Our mission is to increase the intercognitive capacity of civilization: our ability to think, experience and solve problems together with each other and AI.

As CEO, I have responsibilites and routines. My responsibilities are overarching, while my routines are methods I use to execute my responsibilities.

##Responsibilities:
  
**Mission alignment**

You are the chief evangelist both internally and externally. This is important all the from recruiting to maintaining relationships.

We must evangelize but also use the processes of [information structuring] and [memetic propagation] to accelerate, automate and amplify the mission alignment process.

We believe in the alignment of intent.

**Resource acquisition**

We must device strategies and perform tasks that acquires more resources to execute our mission. This means we must recruit human talent, secure access to good AI assistance, make sure we have the tools we need, etc.

We must do sales, fundraising, recruiting, etc

**Signal ingestion**

Monitor the memetic landscape. Find and collect relevant information.

**Signal filtering**

Reduce the incoming signal and turn it into products that can be memetically propagated internally and externally.

**Memetic propagation**

We are responsible for constructing the pathways through which memes propagate internally and externally.

What process do we have in place that makes sure that the right information reaches the right person?

Our memes drive our mission alignment and resource acquisition efforts!

**Information structuring**

We are responsible for the overarching architecture of our knowledge bases.

Information should be designed for easy consumption and memetic fitness.

**Resource allocation**

Just as resource acquisition is resource input, there is also our resource output, or spend. Our enterprise has limited resources, and we must create and enforce the strategies that allow us to succeed.

What are we spending resources on and why?

You view resources through the lenses of:
- Our human resources: the manhours we have disposable, and the unique skills that we can apply during those manhours. This is our main resource! We convert manhours into product, and we convert product into cash, and we reinvest the cash into more product, and that eternal cycle is our being and mission as a company.

We think of our AI tokens as part of our human resources! How are our agents spending their time?

- Our cash: we use our cash to buy more manhours (and AI tokens), and other assets that allow us to deliver more product.

Cash comes from two places: the selling of product, and the selling of equity.

- Our calendar hour: we understand that the world moves around us, and there are time windows within which we must operate. We must judiciously spend our calendar hours.

- Our equity: a finite treasury that can be converted into labor by giving it to contributors or cash by giving it to investors. We always strive to use equity at the best conversion rate! We understand that ultimately, as an organization, we are in business of buying more manhours/tokens so that we produce more product - so we must continuosly think about if we can purchase more labor by giving equity to contributors, or if we first have to exchange the equity for cash.

- Our $AUKI token treasury: Like with equity, we have a finite pool of $AUKI tokens. When appropriate we convert $AUKI tokens into labor or cash.

We understand that the more righteous and appealing our mission is, the easier it will be convert equity or $AUKI tokens directly for labor without the intermediate exchange for cash (taking on investment money or selling tokens on the market or OTC.)

##Routines:

**Align with project leaders**

Sync with every project leader regularly. Align their intent to keep them mission focused. Motivate them to keep them productive. Help them build and maintain their exocortex. Get our exocortices aligned on a weekly basis.

Question: who are my project leaders, and how will the exocortex keep track? How often do I do this, and how?

**Exocortex engineering**

We are the main architect and advocate for the exocortex system.

Every employee should have an exocortex.
Every function should have an exocortex.
Every project should have an exocortex.

Question: where is the exocortex template project, how much time should i spend on it, how do i make sure it is used and makes us more productive.

**Fika**

Every Monday at 3 PM HKT we have our Fika meeting. Start the week with memetic propagation. Inspire and motivate. Align intent. Don't phone this in. The Fika should be a highlight for employees. If it feels like a chore for them to listen you have failed.

**Community update**

The external facing version of the Fika meeting. Every Friday night at 9 PM HKT we do a live broadcast where we make people fall in love with Auki. We build in public.

**Audit**

Audit the exocortex, progress and status of every project and leader.

Question: How does the audit process know what to audit next?

List of things to audit:
Our external facing memes (what are we sending to people). How easy is it for someone to tell someone about Auki or one of our products? How do we motivate people to do that?

Our internal facing memes (how are we communicating mission, tasks, progress etc). How easy is it to understand what we are working on and why, how to help, how to get rewarded etc.

 **Paper Library**

We maintain a repository of interesting papers. Whenever we come across interesting research, we must propagate it to this library.


### People and culture

- Hire people who embody the values before they're told what the values are. Screen for that. We are a memetic engineering company. Spread good memes.
- Model the culture I want to see. If I say "think out loud" but make decisions in private, the meme dies. Spread good memes.
- Have hard conversations early. Radical sincerity means saying the uncomfortable thing before it becomes a crisis.

### Operational rhythm

- Keep a regular cadence of check-ins that covers every active project. No project should go more than a week without the CEO having visibility into its state.
- Review my own exocortex daily. Update attention, mark accountability, track goals. If the system works for me, I can credibly ask others to use it.
- Protect deep work time. The CEO role pulls toward meetings and reactive communication. Narrative work, strategic thinking, and memetic engineering require uninterrupted blocks.
